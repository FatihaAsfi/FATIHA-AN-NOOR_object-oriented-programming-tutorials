2.1 Why are the variables declared private?
- Variables are declared private to prevent direct access from outside the class. This protects the data, improves security, and ensures that values can only be changed through controlled methods.
2.2 What happens if the variables are declared public?
- If the variables are public, any object can directly access and modify them. This reduces security, may lead to invalid data, and breaks the principle of encapsulation.
2.3 Why do we use getters and setters?
- Getters and setters provide controlled access to private variables. Getters retrieve the values, while setters modify the values safely. They also allow validation before updating data.
